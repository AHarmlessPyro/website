Boost Library Official Maintainer Program

Introduction

There are a large number of libraries, still in use and not actively maintained, which have been identified by the Boost Community Maintenance Team. The CMT, however, lacks the resources to properly maintain all of them. To protect the reputation that boost has cultivated over the years, as a organization that provides high quality open source software, it is critical that all libraries bundled with boost releases have active and engaged maintainers. The purpose of this program is to recruit companies and organizations, that are likely providing internal support, to officially take over maintenance of one or more libraries they depend on.

In exchange for maintenance, organizations will be officially recognized by Boost and allowed to display their logo on the Boost Library Official Maintainer site for their library.

Memorandum of Understanding

This Memorandum of Understanding is intended to outline what's expected of library maintainers. By adopting a library, maintainers are explicitly agreeing to provide sufficient resources to acheive the following:

  • Resolve tickets in a timely manner.
  • Support users via email and IRC.
  • Merge pulls requests when appropriate.
  • Support the release team during release preparation.
  • Ensure tests pass on all supported platforms.
  • Maintain documentation.

Exiting the Program

In the chance that maintainers can no longer provide adequate resources required to maintain their library, we ask that they provide a 30-day notice, so that other maintainers can be found. If the Boost committee feels maintainers are not adequately supporting a library, they reserve the right to rescind this agreement with a 30-day notice.
